This is a dice poker game for two or more players who works when the players are in the same party or raid.
The objective is to achieve the best combination in two dice rolls. A normal game is played with six pieces per player.

- Everyone who wants to play bets one coin to join the round, then rolls all 5 dice.
- Each player can announce if they increase the bet by an additional piece and continue the round, or fold and drop out of the round, losing their bet (it stays in the pot).
- Each player still in the round selects the dice they wish to reroll, then rerolls them — or keeps every die as-is, which also counts as their final roll.
- Rolls stay hidden until every player still in the round has locked in their final roll. Results are then revealed for everyone at the same time, and the pot is awarded to the winner automatically.
- If there's a perfect tie between the best hands, the pot is split evenly between the winners (any leftover coin that can't be split evenly stays in the pot for the next round).
- If every player folds after the first roll, no one wins: the pot carries over to the next round instead of being reset.

If you don't have any coins for playing, you can take a (fun) penalty and gain two coins!

List of combinations in order of value:

- Poker: Five dice of the same value
- 4-of-a-Kind: Four dice of the same value
- Full House: A three-of-a-kind + a pair
- Great Straight: Dice sequence of 5 dices
- Small Straight: Dice sequence of 4 dices
- 3-of-a-Kind: Three dice of the same value
- Double Pair: Two pairs
- Pair: Two dice of the same value

When the combinations are of equal value, the one that has the highest dice is declared the winner!
